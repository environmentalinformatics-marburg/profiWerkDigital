That linguistics is a difficult subject for most first-year students becomes apparent fairly early in their first semester. There are three reasons for this. The fact that linguistics is usually not taught at school is the first reason. Literary or cultural studies have a more privileged status in this respect. Teachers teach these subjects in school. The fact that students have to learn many new terms in linguistics is the second reason. It is very demanding to get a grip on all the new concepts. The abstract nature of linguistics is another reason. Often students are scared off by this. To include aspects of linguistics into English language teaching at school would be a promising way of addressing these three problem areas.
