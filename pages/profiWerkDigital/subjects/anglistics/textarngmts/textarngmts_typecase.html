The linguistics behind this
Already over 100 years ago, linguists formulated the idea that each sentence should begin with something that is known to the reader and then move on to something that is new. This has been termed the given-before-new principle. If you look at the original text, you see that many sentences start with new information and that given or known information occurs at the end. In your revised version, you have moved those known bits (closer) to the beginning of the sentence. In the new version, the sentences all follow the given-before-new principle.

These are possible sentence structures that you could use. For instance, sometimes you might want to use a passive clause instead of an active one, but maybe you might want to change a passive to an active clause!
